Remitflex is built for teams moving value **into, across, and out of African markets**. Stablecoins are the fast settlement layer; local bank accounts are the last mile for recipients who need fiat.

One API covers both — you do not stitch together separate providers for crypto collection and bank payouts.

## Why stablecoins for Africa

Traditional cross-border flows into Africa are slow, opaque, and expensive. Stablecoins offer:

- **24/7 settlement** — no cut-off times or weekend delays
- **Transparent fees** — quoted FX and on-chain costs, not hidden correspondent charges
- **Programmable addresses** — generate a deposit route or pay link per customer or invoice
- **Treasury control** — hold USDC and pay out locally when needed

## Typical flows

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Local bank payout">
    Your treasury holds USDC. Create an **offramp** with the recipient's bank details, fund the order, and Remitflex disburses NGN (or another supported currency) to their account.

    Best for: supplier payments, payroll, remittance to non-crypto recipients.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Fiat to stablecoin">
    A user wants to fund a crypto wallet from their local bank. Create an **onramp** — they deposit to the virtual account details you show them, and stablecoin arrives at `destinationAddress`.

    Best for: on-ramping users, prefunding wallets, collecting local currency before treasury conversion.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Diaspora collection">
    A user abroad holds USDC. Create a **payment route** or **payment link** to collect from any supported chain and deliver to your wallet — or follow with an offramp to pay a local bank account.

    Best for: crypto-native pay-ins, marketplace aggregation, invoice collection.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Putting it together

Many products combine naturally:

1. **Discover** supported networks — [Networks & corridors](/concepts/networks-and-corridors)
2. **Collect globally** with a payment link or route
3. **Hold** in stablecoin treasury
4. **Pay locally** with an offramp to a bank account
5. **Reconcile** everything in `GET /v1/transactions`

See [Local fiat](/products/fiat-rails) for offramp and onramp details, and [Payment links](/products/payment-links) for hosted collection.
