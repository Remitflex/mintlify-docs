Stablecoins are blockchain tokens pegged to fiat (usually USD or EUR). RemitFlex uses them as the settlement layer so you get fast, programmable money movement without building wallet or exchange infrastructure yourself.

## Why stablecoins for cross-border

| Traditional wires | Stablecoins via RemitFlex |
|-------------------|---------------------------|
| 1–5 business days | Seconds to minutes on-chain |
| Opaque correspondent fees | Quoted FX + transparent on-chain fees |
| Bank hours only | 24/7 |
| Hard to automate | REST API + deposit addresses |

## What RemitFlex supports today

| Product | Assets |
|---------|--------|
| **Swaps** | USDC, EURC, and other supported pairs on Solana |
| **Payment routes** | Stablecoins on supported origin/destination chains |

Check live support before integrating:

```bash
# Solana swap pairs
curl https://api.remitflex.io/v1/rates/currencies \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $REMITFLEX_API_KEY"

# Cross-chain corridors
curl https://api.remitflex.io/v1/payment-routes/corridors \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $REMITFLEX_API_KEY"
```

## Common stablecoins in African flows

**USDC** — Circle-issued USD stablecoin. Default for treasury and diaspora pay-ins. Broad liquidity on Solana and EVM chains.

**EURC** — Circle-issued EUR stablecoin. Useful for European partners without a USD→EUR conversion step.

**USDT** — Available on some corridors depending on chain liquidity. Check `/payment-routes/currencies` for your route.

## What you do not manage

RemitFlex handles deposit addresses, on-chain execution, and status tracking. Your integration:

1. Creates a swap or payment route (optionally tied to a [customer](/products/customers))
2. Shares the deposit address with the payer (or funds it yourself)
3. Polls status or reads the [transactions ledger](/products/transactions)

See [Swaps](/products/swaps) and [Payment routes](/products/payment-routes) for integration details.
